I want to share a recent remote consulting experience that reminded me why rapid prototyping is such a game-changer.

When you’re working with clients on mobile apps, getting clarity on design early is crucial. The prototype phase isn’t just important – it’s where you either nail the user experience or spend months fixing it later.

The Tool Doesn’t Make the Prototype

Sure, there are tons of dedicated prototyping tools out there. But I’ve seen brilliant prototypes built with PowerPoint, Keynote, even Word documents. If you’re a designer with Sketch skills or know HTML and CSS, you’re definitely ahead of the game.

But real-world consulting? It’s messier than that.

The Challenge

Here’s what the client sent me initially:

Static mockups are fine for initial discussions, but they don’t answer the important questions: How does navigation actually feel? What happens when users tap buttons? How do screens flow together?

To move from “cool idea” to “here’s what it’ll cost and when we can deliver,” I needed something interactive. Something the client could actually use and provide meaningful feedback on.

Enter Ionic Creator

Ionic Creator became my secret weapon for this project.

Creator is a simple drag-&-drop tool for going from the idea to App Store, with just the drag of a mouse

What made it perfect for rapid prototyping was its simplicity. Creator comes loaded with all the Ionic UI elements you need – tabs, side menus, slide boxes, modals, forms, the works.

15 Minutes to Interactive Prototype

Here’s what I built in about 15 minutes:

The difference in client reaction was night and day. Instead of squinting at static screens trying to imagine the flow, they could actually interact with the app. Suddenly, conversations shifted from “I think this might work” to “when users tap here, it should do this instead.”

That’s the power of interactive prototypes – they turn abstract discussions into concrete feedback.

Why This Approach Works

The client experience becomes infinitely better when they can actually touch and feel the product. It feels professional, polished, and gives them confidence that you understand their vision.

But here’s the real win: it saves everyone time and money. Issues that would’ve cost days to fix in development get caught and resolved in minutes during the prototype phase.

Getting Started

The beautiful thing about tools like Creator? The barrier to entry is almost zero:

  • Computer with internet access
  • Basic drag-and-drop skills
  • That’s it

No coding knowledge required. No design degree needed. Just the willingness to iterate quickly and gather feedback early.

The lesson? Don’t underestimate the power of getting something interactive in front of your client as quickly as possible. It’s not about perfection – it’s about communication and alignment. And sometimes, 15 minutes of prototyping saves you 15 weeks of rework.
